Speed Ladder速度阶梯 ⚡ 50 → 180k t/s

Every number on the right is measured: same Mac, the smaller the model, the faster it spits out tokens——but the weaker it gets too. The counters below race in real time at each model's real speed, so you can see the 3,700× gap with your own eyes. The point isn't "who's fastest," it's the trade-off between speed and capability——and how fast the chip has to push the "useful end."右边每个数字都是实测的：同一台 Mac，模型越小，token 吐得越快——但能力也越弱。下面的计数器按各自真实速度实时狂飙，你能直观看到 3700 倍的差距。关键不是"谁最快"，是速度和能力的取舍，以及芯片要把"有用的那端"推多快。
How to read this: the further down, the faster——but the smaller and "emptier" the model. The 50 t/s one can chat and reason; the 180k t/s one is almost a hollow shell, just spitting characters with no real skill.
Our Qwen0.5B (147 t/s) is the smallest point on this line that is "both real and useful"——it can give genuine opinions (that's it speaking inside agora_real.html).
The chip's mission: using ternary-at-training-time + on-chip residency, push a useful model like 0.5B into the high-speed zone on the right (~thousands of t/s) at low power——real and fast at once.
