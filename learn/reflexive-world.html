Three interactives: (1) How much information is actually here to dig out? (2) The reflexivity trap (correlation will fool you). (3) The counterfactual you can never run (parallel worlds).三个互动:① 这里有多少信息可挖? ② 反身性陷阱(看相关性会骗你) ③ 你永远跑不了的那个反事实(平行世界)。
Honest boundary: this is an abstract toy world, used only to demonstrate the shape of "counterfactual / reflexivity" reasoning. It cannot, and does not try to, judge any real history or any real person (family / firm / policy are all abstract settings). In simulation we can run two branches; in reality you only have one timeline — and that is precisely the point.诚实边界:这是一个抽象玩具世界,只用来演示「反事实/反身性」推理的形状。它不能、也不试图评判任何真实历史或真实的人(家庭/公司/政策都是抽象档位)。在仿真里我们能跑两个分支;现实里你只有一条线——这恰恰是要点。

(1) How much information is actually here to dig out?① 这里到底有多少信息可挖?

From "god's-eye view (fully knowable)" to "pure noise (nobody can do anything)". Drag the knobs to see how it splits into "recoverable | the model knows but it's hard to mine | pure noise", and who wins at this amount of information (RAG retrieval / linear model / structured HDC).从"上帝视角(全可知)"到"纯噪声(谁也没辙)"。拖动旋钮看「可恢复 | 模型知道但难挖 | 纯噪声」如何切分,以及在这个信息量下谁赢(RAG检索 / 线性模型 / HDC结构化)。

(2) The reflexivity trap: correlation will fool you② 反身性陷阱:看相关性会骗你

An intervention that "only acts in a crisis" (a bailout / discipline / market rescue). In the data it is positively correlated with "bad things" (because it deliberately steps in during crises), so it looks harmful — but the truth lies in the counterfactual. The smaller the scale and the more you are an insider, the stronger the reflexive kickback.一个"危机时才出手"的干预(救助/管教/救市)。它在数据上和"坏事"正相关(因为它专挑危机出手),看起来有害——但真相要看反事实。尺度越小、你越是局内人,反身反弹就越强。

Click a button above.点上面的按钮。

(3) The counterfactual you can never run (parallel worlds)③ 你永远跑不了的那个反事实(平行世界)

The same intervention, run with the same luck (common random numbers) across N parallel worlds, "with vs without". Look at the distribution of the effect, not a single number. The "one timeline you actually lived" is highlighted — reality only gives you one line.同一个干预,用相同的运气(common random numbers)跑 N 个平行世界的「有 vs 无」。看效果的分布,而不是一个数。高亮"你实际只活过的那一条"——现实只给你一条线。

Click "Run parallel worlds".点"跑平行世界"。

In one line一句话

The person digging wells in the desert is the most diligent and also the poorest — first look at where the water is (1). When you yourself are also playing cards at the table, replaying correlation alone leads to the opposite conclusion — you must run small, reversible experiments (2). Reality gives you only one line, you cannot run the parallel worlds — so the smaller and more irreversible it is, the more you must rely on principles (priors), not on "what happened to me" (3).在沙漠里挖井的人最勤奋,也最穷——先看哪里有水(①)。 你自己也在桌上打牌时,光复盘相关性会得出反的结论——必须做小而可逆的实验(②)。 现实只给你一条线,平行世界你跑不了——所以越是小、越不可逆,越要靠原则(先验),而不是"我身上发生了什么"(③)。
